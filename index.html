Original Research

Building at the frontier of
speech AI and accessibility

My M.Tech research at DA-IICT focuses on speaker verification for people with dysarthria — a motor speech disorder that causes significant deviation from the healthy speech distributions that most speaker models are trained on. The challenge: build systems that are robust, cross-lingual, and deployable in real healthcare contexts.

Neural Backend Speaker Verification for Dysarthric Speech

Proposed a learned similarity model using ECAPA-TDNN embeddings with neural scoring to outperform cosine similarity baselines under pathological, cross-lingual conditions.

IEEE · In preparation

About me

Researcher who writes.
Writer who builds.

I have completed my Masters in Machine Learning from DA-IICT Gandhinagar, researching speaker verification for dysarthric speech. I work with deep speaker embedding models (ECAPA-TDNN, CAM++), design cross-lingual evaluation pipelines in PyTorch, and think carefully about how AI can work for people at the margins of what current systems were built for.

My writing comes from that same place: I want to explain not just what systems do but why they're designed the way they are, where they break, and what the engineering tradeoffs actually mean. I've published technical case studies and tutorials, and I'm working toward original research writing that can bridge the lab and the broader technical community.

I'm a Harvard WEAmplify Scholar, GATE CSE top 3.54%, and a Microsoft Learn Student Ambassador.
